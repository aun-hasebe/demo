Airport to Downtown: How to Ride the Limousine Bus and Save Money

No transfers, no wrestling with luggage. Here's how to book, board, and get the best fares — plus how to line up your next tickets before you land.

What Is the Limousine Bus? How It's Different From the Train

The Limousine Bus runs straight from the airport arrivals hall to major train stations and hotels — no transfers required. Unlike the train, you won't be hauling a heavy suitcase through ticket gates and up stairs; you just take a seat and ride to your stop. The driver stores your bags in the hold, so getting around is easy even if you don't know the area.

Direct, No Transfers

Board at arrivals and ride straight to major hotels and stations on a single bus.

Just Check Your Suitcase

Hand it to the driver and take your seat — no need to haul luggage down the aisle.

Drops You at Major Hotels

Many routes stop at several hotels, so you can pick the stop closest to where you're staying.

3 Steps to Riding the Bus

Here's the whole process from booking to arrival, broken down so first-timers won't get lost.

1

Book Your Ticket

Book online or at an airport counter, choosing your destination and departure time. Same-day tickets are often available if seats remain, but booking ahead is safer for early-morning, late-night, and holiday departures.

2

Find Your Stop and Wait

Follow the signs from the arrivals hall to your boarding stop. On high-frequency routes, you'll rarely wait more than a few tens of minutes for the next bus.

3

Arrive and Collect Your Luggage

Once you reach your stop, just grab your bag from the hold — that's it. On routes with multiple hotel stops, it's worth confirming your stop in advance so you get off at the right one.

3 Ways to Get More for Your Money

How you book can matter as much as which route you take.

ROUND-TRIP

Book a round-trip fare

A round-trip ticket is often cheaper than buying two one-way fares, depending on the route.

BOOK EARLY

Reserve online ahead of time

Once your travel date is set, book online early — some routes offer discounted advance fares.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to book in advance?
Same-day tickets are often available at the counter if seats remain, but early-morning, late-night, holiday, and peak-season departures can sell out — booking ahead is the safer choice.
How many suitcases can I check?
It depends on the route, but most allow 1–2 large bags per passenger free of charge. If you're traveling with extra luggage, check the official rules in advance.
What payment methods are accepted?
Credit cards, IC transit cards, and cash are all commonly accepted, though this varies by route and where you buy your ticket. Most major credit cards are accepted for online booking.
What if I'm not sure which stop to get off at?
Your list of stops is shown when you book, so check the one closest to your hotel in advance. You can also confirm with the driver or the in-bus display once you're on board.
